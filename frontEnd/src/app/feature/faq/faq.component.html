Frequently Asked Questions

Answers for Consumers

General Questions

What makes OLF different?

Farmhouse eliminates the middleman. When you buy something on Farmhouse you are actually purchasing directly from the Farmer/Producer. Just like a physical farmers’ market, the farmer sets up their stall and sets their product prices, you purchase directly from the farmer, your money goes directly to the farmer and then the farmer packages and posts your products direct to you. Keep in mind that you will receive a package from each farmer you order from and that could be from anywhere in Australia.

What area is served? What exactly is local? Where does the food come from?

The beauty of an online space for local food is that the area served is whatever area a producer wants to serve. Producers could potentially come from anywhere. You get to decide how ‘local’ you want local to be. Our aim is simply to make things as local as possible.

What makes OLF so different from other online food delivery services?
  • In short, we don’t touch the food. We don’t deliver. We don’t ship.
  • Virtually every other local food website out there is a retailer/distributor. They buy the food from producers, and sell to the consumer. This adds a lot of cost to the transaction which can mean producers from the producer. They are already doing deliveries and attending markets, we just making finding and ordering easier for you.

Ordering

Are there delivery fees?

There could be. Door-to-door deliveries could have a fee attached to them, if the producer chooses. And that fee may apply to all orders, or it may be waived when a certain order value is reached. All this information will be available to see in the schedule details.

As a consumer, why do I need to sign up? Why can't I use it as a guest?

Benefits: passive – information comes to you. No need to hunt. Don’t miss out. Discover new vendors/products. Plan ahead.

What is available?

Well, that depends. OLF is producer-driven, meaning that we don’t control or manipulate what is available. Availability will depend on what producers choose to sell and what’s in season.

How do I get my order? When will I get my order?

How you get your order depends on the choice you made as you checked out. Producers set up their own schedules and can choose:

  • door-to-door deliveries: the producer comes to your location (an address and instructions will need to be added at checkout),
  • market pickups (your order can be picked up at the producer’s stall at a farmers’ market or roadside stall),
  • on-farm pickups (you will go to the producer’s location to get your order), or
  • off-farm pickups (you will pick your order up at another, pre-chosen location – perhaps a butcher shop or other delivery point).

When you get your order is also chosen at checkout time based on the producer’s schedule. We’d suggest that you make a note of the date and time in your calendar as you check out.

How do I pay?

You pay your producer directly when you get your order. When you checkout, you will be asked to provide how you will pay based on the choices given by the producer. The current options are credit card, debit card, e-transfer, cash, and cheque.

What if there’s nothing for sale in my area?

This is going to happen at some point. If you visit OLF from Manitoba in the middle of January, you might not see much if anything available. If you’d like to be notified when something is scheduled in your area, sign up to our Location Notifications. You choose a location or locations, and when a delivery or pickup is scheduled there, you’ll get an email. No need to keep checking back. We’ll do the searching for you.
